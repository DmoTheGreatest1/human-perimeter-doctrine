=====================================================================
Human Perimeter Doctrine (HPD)
State & Institutional FAQ
=====================================================================

Status: CANONICAL · NON-PROPRIETARY · NON-ENFORCING
Audience: State Agencies, Public Utilities, CIO/CTO, CFO,
          Procurement, Legal Counsel

---------------------------------------------------------------------
1. What is HPD, in one sentence?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD is a documentation-first governance framework that preserves
human decision authority while improving reconciliation, settlement,
and audit visibility in decision-bearing workflows.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Is HPD a product, service, or software platform?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

No.

HPD provides:
- documentation
- architectural standards
- reconciliation logic
- human-decision boundaries

HPD does NOT provide:
- hosted services
- enforcement tools
- monitoring agents
- compliance software
- operational control

---------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Is HPD regulatory or punitive?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

No.

HPD does not:
- impose penalties
- levy fines
- trigger enforcement
- mandate corrective action
- initiate clawbacks

HPD only documents whether evidence reconciles.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
4. What problem does HPD solve for states?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD addresses accounting and reconciliation friction in programs
where public funds depend on performance claims.

Examples:
- broadband subsidies
- infrastructure grants
- energy efficiency programs
- capacity provisioning
- vendor performance payments

The problem is usually not fraud.
It is slow, adversarial verification.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Does HPD replace existing systems?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

No.

HPD does not replace:
- billing systems
- network operations
- procurement workflows
- financial systems
- regulatory authority

HPD operates out-of-band as a reconciliation layer.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
6. What data does HPD require?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD requires only cryptographic summaries.

Typical inputs:
- batch hashes of logs
- signed receipts
- bounded telemetry summaries

HPD does NOT require:
- raw traffic inspection
- customer data
- packet payloads
- continuous monitoring

Raw logs remain local to the operator.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
7. What is "Ring-0"?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Ring-0 is a human finalization boundary.

It activates only when automated reconciliation fails.

Characteristics:
- binary decisions (ratify / reject)
- mandatory written rationale
- append-only record
- no negotiation
- no partial settlements

Most users never interact with Ring-0.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Who controls Ring-0 decisions?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

The adopting institution.

HPD does not:
- appoint reviewers
- certify personnel
- supervise decisions
- override outcomes

Institutions retain full authority and responsibility.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Is HPD free?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD is free for:
- reading
- citation
- policy discussion
- academic use
- legislative reference
- forking and critique

No permission or payment is required for speech.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
10. When does funding apply?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Funding applies only when an institution relies on HPD outputs
to make or justify real decisions involving:

- financial settlement
- resource allocation
- escrow timing
- payment release or delay

This is use-based, not access-based.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
11. Who sets pricing or compensation?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

The institution.

HPD:
- does not set prices
- does not invoice
- does not collect fees
- does not negotiate terms
- does not enforce payment

Funding resolution occurs through existing institutional processes.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
12. What is stewardship compensation?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Stewardship refers to the human labor that maintains HPD as a
coherent public framework, including:

- authorship
- canonical maintenance
- boundary definition
- hash anchoring
- reference architecture

Recognition of this labor does not create ownership or control.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
13. Is this vendor lock-in?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

No.

HPD is:
- non-proprietary
- fork-safe
- documentation-based
- non-exclusive

Institutions can exit at any time without penalty.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
14. How does HPD improve efficiency?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

By:
- reducing audit cycles
- accelerating clean settlements
- making discrepancies visible early
- minimizing retroactive disputes

Efficiency gains often exceed implementation cost.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
15. What about energy and capacity efficiency?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD makes inefficiency visible.

As a secondary effect, institutions can identify:
- over-provisioned capacity
- under-utilized infrastructure
- energy spent without acknowledged service

HPD does not mandate changes.
It reveals options.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
16. Is HPD legally binding?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

No.

HPD does not create legal obligations.
It documents evidence and decisions.

Institutions determine how records are used.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
17. What happens if HPD is ignored or misused?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Nothing happens procedurally.

The record simply reflects:
- what was reconciled
- what was not
- who decided

Visibility is the accountability mechanism.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
18. What does a successful pilot look like?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

- faster settlements
- fewer audits
- clearer records
- reduced political friction
- improved planning data

---------------------------------------------------------------------
19. Is HPD suitable for federal or interstate use?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Yes.

HPD is jurisdiction-agnostic and non-coercive.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
20. Where does HPD stop?
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD stops at documentation.

It does not:
- govern people
- enforce behavior
- optimize outcomes
- override institutions

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Summary
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD allows states to reconcile money, performance, and reality
without surveillance, punishment, or operational disruption.
