Human Perimeter Doctrine (HPD)
Procurement-Safe Cost Appendix
=====================================================================

Status: CANONICAL · NON-PROPRIETARY · NON-BINDING
Audience: Procurement, Finance, Budget, Audit, Legal

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose
---------------------------------------------------------------------

This appendix clarifies the cost posture of HPD for institutional
adopters. It is descriptive, not prescriptive.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Cost Classification
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD is not a licensed product or subscription service.

Costs arise only if an institution elects to rely on HPD outputs
within a decision-bearing workflow.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Eligible Cost Categories (Illustrative)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

If operational reliance is chosen, institutions typically budget for:

1. Internal Personnel
   - reviewers
   - analysts
   - decision officers
   - audit or reconciliation staff

2. Operational Infrastructure
   - logging retention
   - evidence storage
   - cryptographic tooling
   - reconciliation interfaces

3. Stewardship Recognition
   - compensation acknowledging authorship and maintenance of
     the HPD framework
   - resolved through existing procurement or mediated processes

---------------------------------------------------------------------
What HPD Does Not Require
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD does NOT require:

- licensing fees
- per-transaction pricing
- revenue sharing
- vendor exclusivity
- proprietary software purchases

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Pricing Authority
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD does not set:

- rates
- minimums
- schedules
- payment terms
- compensation formulas

All funding decisions are made solely by the adopting institution.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Cost Neutrality and Savings
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Institutions commonly realize:

- reduced audit costs
- fewer retroactive disputes
- faster settlement cycles
- improved planning data
- visibility into over-provisioned capacity

These effects may produce net savings.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Procurement Compatibility
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD is compatible with:

- RFP processes
- pilot programs
- demonstration authorities
- sole-source justification (if applicable)
- internal budget allocation

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Summary
---------------------------------------------------------------------

HPD does not introduce new mandatory costs.
It reallocates existing reconciliation effort into a visible,
documented, and auditable structure.
